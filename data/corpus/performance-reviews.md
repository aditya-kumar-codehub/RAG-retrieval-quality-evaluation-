# Performance Reviews

Northwind Cloud conducts formal performance reviews twice a year, in January and July. Each review cycle includes a self-assessment, peer feedback from 2-4 colleagues selected by the employee and their manager, and a manager evaluation, culminating in a calibration meeting where managers align ratings across their teams.

Promotions are decided by a promotion committee that meets quarterly, independent of the twice-yearly review cycle — an employee can be promoted at any quarterly committee meeting if their manager submits a promotion packet, though most promotions are timed to align with the January or July review cycle for compensation-planning reasons.

Compensation adjustments tied to performance ratings are applied following the July review cycle only; the January cycle is developmental and does not directly trigger a compensation change, though exceptional January ratings may prompt an off-cycle adjustment at manager discretion.

New employees are not evaluated in their first formal review cycle if they have been employed for less than 90 days at the time of that cycle; they instead receive an informal 90-day check-in with their manager, separate from the formal review process described here.
