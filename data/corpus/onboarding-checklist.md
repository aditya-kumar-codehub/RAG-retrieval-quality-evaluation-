# New Employee Onboarding Checklist

Before an employee's start date, IT ships a company laptop (see `equipment-policy.md`) timed to arrive the business day before start for remote hires, and HR sends a welcome email with login credentials for the HR portal, payroll system, and benefits enrollment.

On day one, new employees complete: (1) I-9 or right-to-work verification, (2) benefits enrollment initiation (final elections are due within 30 days, per `benefits-overview.md`), (3) laptop setup including MDM enrollment and full-disk encryption, and (4) an introductory meeting with their manager to review 30/60/90-day goals.

Within the first 2 weeks, new employees must complete mandatory security and compliance training, as described in `security-policy.md`, and acknowledge the Code of Conduct (`code-of-conduct.md`). Remote employees should also claim their one-time $500 home office equipment stipend during this window, following the process in `remote-work-policy.md`.

At the 90-day mark, new employees have an informal check-in with their manager rather than a formal performance review, per `performance-reviews.md`, and — for hybrid roles — become eligible for full-remote status per `remote-work-policy.md`.
